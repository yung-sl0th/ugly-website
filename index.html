The Eternal in Spanish Art

Spanish art has long reflected the human experience of cyclical existence—birth, death, decay, and renewal—through deeply emotional, surreal, and spiritual themes. From Goya’s bleak reflections on war and death, to Dalí’s dreamy landscapes of distorted time, to Gaudí’s living architecture, artists have explored the eternal not as something fixed, but as something evolving, looping, and reborn. These works do not shy away from darkness—they embrace it as a space where new meaning is created.
